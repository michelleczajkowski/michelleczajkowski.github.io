The Study

My PhD project is grounded in Radboud's RADAr test. The RADAr aims to offer diagnostic information to students on their academic language skills. Diagnostic tests aim to offer feedback that can be used to improve weak areas; therefore feedback must be as fine grained, relevant, and actionable as possible.

A key issue in the diagnostic assessment of writing is that, generally, only the written product is accessible in test design and rating, yet most instruction on writing takes a process-approach. This mismatch exists because the writing process has traditionally been quite inaccessible. However this has begun to rapidly change. Consider proctor software which routinely records the writing process. Consider that some students make records of the writing process to show the product is their own work. It is now a good time to explore the diagnostic potential that greater accessibility to the writing process offers.
