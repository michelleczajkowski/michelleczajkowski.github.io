The Study: The Diagnostic "Added Value" of Observing the Writing Process

A key issue in the diagnostic assessment of writing is that, generally, only the written product is accessible to assessors and teachers. In this study, I ask:

Would having access to the process add value to a diagnostic assessment of students' writing?

Data Collected

As part of today's workshop I will be collecting data. You will be asked to score tasks and to write notes and comments. These will be collected the old fashioned way - on paper. Please write clearly :D

Your input will be anonymous.


Goals:
To collect judgements on a sample of student academic texts.
To look behind the scenes to see how problems in the text arise in the writing process.
To introduce some useful software and methodology for teachers of writing.
